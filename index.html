04

Our vision

01 / 07

Paradise garden

A garden of paradise — first and always

The name is a promise: Gardens of Eden is paradise. Every frame — a lobby, a bedroom, a reel — has to feel green, calm and alive. Lose that feeling and it is just another Phuket condo.

Paradise garden

The garden is the hero, not the building

Gardens of Eden has what almost no one here has: 30+ acres, a park, a lake, 50 meters to the beach and no cars on the ground. So we shoot the buildings as part of the garden — not the garden as a strip of green between blocks.

Premium

Quiet luxury, not loud luxury

Real premium is felt, not announced. It lives in space, privacy, water, natural materials and light — and in a team that fixes things before you notice. Never in gold and marble shouting for attention.

Premium

Premium lives in the places, not the price

Six restaurants, the lagoon, the wellness club, the interiors, the infrastructure — we film every place as somewhere you already want to be, not as a line on an amenity list.

Cinematic difference

We don't shoot ads. We make cinema.

This is what makes Eden special. Even a 15-second reel is a tiny film — a title card, a mini-plot, a world with its own concierge. We make you feel the place is unlike anything else, without selling head-on.

Cinematic difference

The little things that make it a film

A raised eyebrow, a sunbed lined up a touch too perfectly, a mango that returns a scene later, a locked frame that breathes. We earn the feeling with craft — we never announce it.

All three

Three pillars, one world

Paradise garden, quiet premium, cinema — every format stands on the same three. Website, showroom, reels, VSL, broker decks, ads: different formats, one hand behind them.

See it → feel it → trust it → buy it → tell someone.

05

Flagship · game film 2:00 — concept

01 / 03

Game film · 2:00 · 16:9

CONCIERGE

A distinguished Thai concierge answers one question in two minutes — “What is Gardens of Eden?” He leads us through the garden, the home, the kitchen and the island, helping with his own hands in every story.

But the answer is born not from his words — from the life around him. A dog carries off a ball, a child counts the steps to the sea, a chef slips him a spoon of soup. Not an ad — dozens of living scenes, and from them, like a mosaic, the answer takes shape.

Each scene quietly shows one advantage — not a line from a brochure, but a moment that makes you smile. Silence, space, the sea nearby, care, taste, privacy — lived, not told.

Chapter I · 0:14–0:33

THE GARDEN

Seventy percent of the land is garden — the houses hide in the canopy, and the sea waits at the edge.

  • Dawn from above: homes drowned in treetops, the paths like the veins of a leaf.
  • The dog's own POV — a ball bounces down a tunnel of palms, and not a single car in sight.
  • A lady swims the infinity edge in a wide-brimmed hat; the brim stays perfectly dry.
  • A white-gloved hand slips in, makes one move on the chessboard, and leaves.

“Seventy percent of this land is garden. The rest, we tried to keep out of its way.”

Chapter II · 0:33–0:57

THE HOME

This is where people live, not vacation — and the loudest luxury here is silence.

  • A doll-house cutaway: a birthday leaps on the sofa upstairs, while below the tea sits perfectly still.
  • The concierge catches the school shuttle and hands a forgotten lunchbox through the window — before she remembers it.
  • A barefoot boy counts his steps to the sea: “…forty-nine, fifty!”
  • The car dives into the garage; below, dad steps straight into the private brass lift — “5”, home.

“Silence is the only luxury you cannot photograph. So we built it into the walls.”

Chapters III–IV · 0:57–1:45

THE TABLE & THE ISLAND

Ten restaurants and an open sea — the last, warmest turn before the answer.

  • Ten kitchens in a row: a grill throws a column of fire, flour claps into the beat, a knife opens a mango.
  • A wall of tropical rain — the staff open the umbrellas as one; the concierge gives his to a girl and stays in the downpour.
  • The catamaran cuts the turquoise, and from the bow the children scream with joy.
  • A starlit night, a brass telescope; a paper boat drifts into the dark — and dissolves into the mist where it all began.

“Ten restaurants — one for every mood you haven't met yet.”

Technology

HOW WE'LL SHOOT IT

We shoot in a combined way: real people in real places, plus the built world of Gardens of Eden around them.

The living foundation is shot for real. The real concierge, real guests, children, chefs — real textures and real emotion. Everything already finished is filmed on site and in the completed showrooms, so the frame holds real life, not a picture.

And whatever is still under construction, we build out with modern technology and place our living heroes into the finished world of Eden — the gardens, the pools, the terraces, the sea, as the project will be at hand-over. The viewer sees no construction and no bare render, but a whole, warm world already alive.
